Confidence is meant to move. After a mistake, after a quiet game, after a long stretch of training without a result. It dips, you do the work to rebuild it, and it comes back.

What is different is when low confidence stops being about sport and starts spreading into your mood. When you stop enjoying things you used to love, when you feel flat for weeks at a time, when getting out of bed feels heavier than it should. That is not a confidence problem. That is your mood asking for something more.

## What this can look like

- A heaviness that does not lift even after a good performance.
- Loss of interest in things outside sport: hobbies, friends, food.
- Tiredness that sleep does not fix.
- Feeling worthless, hopeless, or like a burden to others.
- Difficulty concentrating, remembering things, or making small decisions.

## When it crosses the line

A useful framing comes from the diagnostic criteria for depression: low mood OR loss of interest, plus other symptoms, present most of the day, most days, for at least two weeks. If that describes the last fortnight, the right step is not another confidence drill. It is to talk to someone.

The cleanest distinction: low confidence is usually situational and shifts with feedback. Low mood is global and does not lift even when good things happen.

## What helps

- Move your body, even gently. Exercise has consistent evidence for reducing depressive symptoms (Schuck et al. 2016).
- Keep one small daily anchor: a walk, a phone call, a journal line. Routine protects mood.
- Limit alcohol. It tends to deepen low mood the day after.
- Tell one person. Saying it out loud removes some of the weight.

## Where to get professional support

- **Your GP** is the first port of call. They can assess your mood and offer treatment options.
- **NHS Talking Therapies (UK)**: free CBT, counselling, and other talking therapies.
- **HSE Mental Health Services (Ireland)**: community mental health teams via GP referral.
- **Mind (UK)** at mind.org.uk for understanding depression and what helps.
- **Aware (Ireland)** at aware.ie for depression support, including free support groups.

## What I have learned

For a long time I treated my low mood as a confidence problem. I tried to train harder out of it, talk myself out of it, push through. That did not work because it was not actually a confidence problem. The day I went to my doctor and described what I was actually feeling was the day things started to change. Medication, talk therapy, and the simple act of being honest about it were the turning points.

## Crisis resources

- **Samaritans** (UK and IE): 116 123 — 24/7 free listening line.
- **Pieta House** (IE): 1800 247 247 — suicide and self-harm support.
- If you are in immediate danger: **999 (UK)** or **112 (IE)**.
