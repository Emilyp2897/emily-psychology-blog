Every team has conflict. Disagreements about tactics, frustrations after a loss, friction between players who see the game differently. That is normal, and the best teams have a way of moving through it.

What is different is when a team environment starts to feel unsafe. When you stop speaking up because you know you will be ignored or punished. When you start dreading training because of who will be there, not what is being run. When the cost of being on the team is your wellbeing. That is a psychological safety issue, and it can take a serious toll on your mental health.

## What this can look like

- Dreading going to training, not because of the work, but because of the environment.
- Holding back ideas, mistakes, or feedback because of how you think others will react.
- Persistent exclusion, gossip, or being made the target of jokes.
- A coach or senior player who uses fear, shame, or public humiliation as motivation.
- Feeling more anxious, withdrawn, or tearful in the days around team commitments.

## When it crosses the line

A team that has conflict but addresses it openly is healthy. A team where conflict turns into exclusion, intimidation, or sustained bullying is not. Research by Edmondson (1999) defines psychological safety as the shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking. If that has broken down, performance and mental health both suffer.

If team dynamics are affecting your sleep, your mood, or your willingness to be there at all, the issue is no longer just a team issue. It is a wellbeing issue and worth treating that way.

## What helps

- Talk to one person you trust, on or off the team. Naming it out loud often shifts what feels possible.
- Speak to your club welfare officer (most clubs in the LGFA and GAA have one).
- Keep a brief written record of incidents (dates, what happened). It protects your memory and helps if you decide to escalate.
- Reach out to your county or club Health and Wellbeing committee for support.

## Where to get professional support

- **Your GP** if dynamics are affecting your mood or anxiety.
- **NHS Talking Therapies (UK)** for support processing what has happened.
- **HSE Mental Health Services (Ireland)** via GP referral.
- **LGFA Player Welfare** and **GPA (Gaelic Players Association)** for player-specific support and welfare officers.
- **ACAS (UK)** at acas.org.uk if a workplace dynamic crosses into the workplace.

## What I have learned

A team can be the best thing in your life and a hard thing in your life at the same time. What I had to learn was that staying quiet about something that was hurting me was not loyalty, it was self-abandonment. The people who care about you and the sport will want to know.

## Crisis resources

- **Samaritans** (UK and IE): 116 123 — 24/7 free listening line.
- **Pieta House** (IE): 1800 247 247 — suicide and self-harm support.
- If you are in immediate danger: **999 (UK)** or **112 (IE)**.
