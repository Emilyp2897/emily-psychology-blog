Month 12 toolkit. Final toolkit of the 12-month series. Paired with [Understanding Off-Season Mental Health](/content-hub/training-the-mind/understanding-off-season-mental-health/) and [Applying Off-Season Recovery in LGFA](/content-hub/training-the-mind/applying-off-season-recovery-in-lgfa/).

Four tools.

## Tool 1: The 6-Week Off-Season Structure

**When to use:** the day after your last competitive match.

**How to do it:**

| Week | Physical | Mental / Social |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Total rest. No structured exercise. | Sleep extra. See family. Eat what feels good. |
| 2 | Light daily movement (walk, swim, easy cycle). | Reconnect with one non-squad friend. |
| 3 | Walks plus one yoga or mobility session. | Pick a non-sport activity for the off-season. |
| 4 | Light aerobic 2-3 times. Easy strength. | Sleep and eat at consistent times. Social rhythm. |
| 5 | Build aerobic. Add easy ball work. | Continue routine. Squad social event if possible. |
| 6 | Light pre-pre-season work. Ramping up. | Mental prep for pre-season starting. |

Adjust based on your specific pre-season start date.

**Why it works:** the structure protects both physical and mental recovery. Most athletes either rest too long (decondition) or not enough (don't actually recover). This balances both.

## Tool 2: The Off-Season Identity Anchor

**When to use:** week 1 of the off-season.

**How to do it:**

Write down answers to:

1. **Who am I when I'm not playing this week?**
2. **What do I want to do with this off-season that I can't do during the season?**
3. **What's one relationship I want to invest in over the next 6 weeks?**
4. **What's one non-sport activity I want to give time to?**
5. **What kind of athlete (and person) do I want to be when I come back to pre-season?**

Keep the answers somewhere visible. Read them when the off-season feels weird (which it will).

**Why it works:** the off-season is hardest for athletes with high athletic identity (most ladies Gaelic players). Pre-committing to non-sport identity anchors helps you not drift into restlessness or low mood.

## Tool 3: The Pre-Pre-Season Build

**When to use:** the 2 weeks before pre-season starts.

**How to do it:**

Build aerobic and basic strength back up GRADUALLY.

- 2-3 aerobic sessions per week. 30-45 minutes. Low-moderate intensity.
- Light gym 2 times per week. Compound movements, sub-max loads.
- Re-introduce some short, easy ball work in week 2.
- Sleep 7-9 hours consistently.
- Eat properly. Fuel coming back up.

What NOT to do:
- Test yourself. No max efforts. No timed 5ks.
- Try to be the fittest on Day 1 of pre-season.
- Make up for "lost time" if you took the early weeks easy. There's nothing to make up.

**Why it works:** abrupt jumps into pre-season conditioning are the leading cause of early-season soft-tissue injuries. A gradual ramp gives the tissues time to adapt and reduces injury risk significantly. Sport science research is consistent on this.

## Tool 4: The "Are You Okay?" Self-Check

**When to use:** at week 3 of the off-season.

**How to do it:**

Honestly answer:

1. **Sleep:** is it normal or significantly disrupted?
2. **Mood:** is it flat-but-okay, or persistently low?
3. **Energy:** is it returning, or still depleted?
4. **Social:** are you connected to people, or withdrawing?
5. **Future:** does the next season feel like something to look forward to, or dread?

If most answers are positive, the off-season is doing its job. You'll come back ready.

If most answers are negative AT WEEK 3, this is worth attention. Possible interpretations:
- Burnout that needs more recovery than the normal off-season allows.
- An identity issue that the season was masking.
- Depression or anxiety that needs professional support.
- Life events outside sport that need attention.

In all cases: talk to someone. Your GP. A sport psychologist. The support contacts on the [resources page](/resources). You can also email Emily directly: emilyphelan@mindthegael.co.uk.

**Why it works:** the off-season is when deeper patterns surface. Catching them at week 3 is far better than catching them at week 8 or letting them quietly affect the next season.

## Putting it together

This is the toolkit you'll come back to year after year if you stay in the sport.

The off-season is its own training discipline. The athletes who do it well come back stronger. The athletes who don't usually find out, eventually, that one of two things happened:

- They burned out and lost their love of the sport.
- They got injured early in the next season because they didn't really recover.

Treat the off-season as seriously as you treat championship week. Different intensity, same care.
