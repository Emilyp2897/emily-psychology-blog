# The Training the Mind Series – Week 1

Welcome to **Week 1** of the *Training the Mind* series.  
This series explores key sports psychology theories and how they relate to **Ladies Gaelic Football**, with practical insights for players, coaches, and teams.

## Posts in this series

1. [Social Identity Theory (SIT)](/blog/training-the-mind/social-identity-theory)  
   How group identity influences players’ motivation, cohesion, and performance.

2. [Self-Categorization Theory (SCT)](/blog/training-the-mind/self-categorization-theory)  
   Understanding individual and team identities, and how they impact behavior on the pitch.

3. [Input-Process-Outcome (IPO) Framework](/blog/training-the-mind/ipo-framework)  
   How inputs, team processes, and outcomes interact in team performance.

4. [Social Facilitation & Inhibition – Drive Theory](/blog/training-the-mind/driver-theory)  
   Why the presence of others can improve or impair performance.

5. [Distraction-Conflict Theory](/blog/training-the-mind/distraction-conflict-theory)  
   How attentional conflict under pressure affects performance.

6. [Collective Sports Team Collapse](/blog/training-the-mind/collective-sport-team-collapse)  
   Understanding why entire teams sometimes fail in high-pressure moments.

7. [Choking Under Pressure](/blog/training-the-mind/choking-under-pressure)  
   Psychological mechanisms behind performance breakdowns.

8. [Attention Control Theory](/blog/training-the-mind/attention-control-theory)  
   How anxiety reduces processing efficiency and working memory.

9. [Processing Efficiency Theory](/blog/training-the-mind/processing-efficiency-theory)  
   Maintaining performance under stress, but at a cognitive cost.

10. [Threat vs Challenge Response](/blog/training-the-mind/threat-vs-challenge)  
    Understanding how athletes perceive and respond to high-pressure situations.

11. [Cognitive Narrowing Under Stress](/blog/training-the-mind/cognitive-narrowing-under-stress)  
    Why focus may become restricted and critical cues are missed under pressure.

12. [Decision Errors in High-Intensity Moments](/blog/training-the-mind/decision-errors-in-high-intensity-moments)  
    How stress and cognitive load contribute to mistakes in crucial game situations.
