Online platform for mental wellbeing & performance coaching for female athletes

1. Executive Summary

Mind the Gael is an online platform delivering productised mental wellbeing and physical training content to female athletes. The business model is direct-to-consumer digital products with very high gross margins, supported by a small fixed-cost stack (Vercel, Postgres, Resend, Anthropic API, Stripe).

The revenue mix sits across three product families: physical training plans (one-off purchases), mental performance plans (one-off purchases, parallel architecture to training plans), and a monthly blog subscription. Each purchase costs the business roughly £0.40 to £0.65 in variable cost (API + payment processing), against retail prices of £5 to £20.

The platform is engineered to scale: both plan generators run autonomously after Stripe verification, content is served from a CDN-backed static site, and the live human intervention layer (Emily) is only invoked for clinical-pause cases and the four specialised training tracks (pregnancy, postpartum, endometriosis, return-to-play).

Headline numbers. Per-sale gross margin sits between 94 % and 97 %. Fixed monthly running costs at v1 scale are approximately £19/month (Canva Pro and the annual domain — Claude is on the free tier, hosting and Postgres are free, Resend is free). The business breaks even on ongoing costs at 2 plan sales per month. Total pre-launch investment to recoup is dominated by Anthropic API spend during the build — see Section 1B below for the up-to-date invoice total.

2.2 Mental Performance Plans (live)

A productised offering parallel to the training plans, with shared infrastructure (same intake → teaser preview → Stripe → full plan emailed flow, branched by plan_type in the database). Content backbone is the 12-month Gael Performance Toolkit. The intake captures mental-performance context (competition level, performance moments to work on, current routines, peak moments, struggle moments, confidence level) rather than physical-training context. Pricing mirrors training plans.

ProductPriceDelivery
6-Week Mental Performance Plan£10Self-paced plan delivered by email after Stripe checkout. Six weekly themes drawn from the Gael Performance Toolkit, daily practice tables, routines to build, weekly reflection prompts.
12-Week Mental Performance Plan£20Twelve-week version, spanning the full toolkit across confidence, focus, routines, self-talk, visualisation, motivation, mistake recovery, team pressure, return from injury.

Cross-plan companion linking. Both intake forms include an optional field asking if the client is also doing the other Mind the Gael plan. If they paste a short summary, that context is injected into the generator prompt, so a mental plan can anchor its weekly "performance moment to apply it" to real sessions from the client's physical plan, and a physical plan can layer refocus cues and routine practice into specific training days. The two plans reinforce each other without requiring any cross-database join.

2.4 Team Plans (live)

Squad-wide plans built around a coach's team (not individual players). Coach submits a team intake (sport, season phase, training days, equipment, group size, primary goal), the AI generates one squad plan reviewed by Emily, and the coach receives a private shareable link to send to their players. No individual player medical, cycle, or mental-health data is collected.

Pricing: £8 per head per block. A coach with a 20-player squad pays £160 for a single team plan; a 30-player squad pays £240. The per-head model keeps it accessible for small clubs and scales naturally for large squads. Invoiced via Stripe Invoicing per block while the volume is small; can flip to checkout once volume justifies the integration work.

9. Cost Optimisations Available

  • Prompt caching — Anthropic discounts identical prompt prefixes when used within 5 minutes. Could shave ~50 % off input-token cost. Worth implementing once traffic justifies it.
  • Lower max_tokens caps — current 6,000 and 12,000 token caps are generous. Empirical usage will likely be 30–40 % lower. Caps can be tightened after testing.
  • Cheaper model for teasers — Claude Haiku is ~5× cheaper than Sonnet. The teaser is short and structured; could be moved to Haiku for ~80 % cost reduction on the non-buyer path.
  • Move Postgres to Supabase free tier if Neon (Vercel Postgres) pricing becomes unfavourable at scale.

11. Pricing Strategy Notes

Why £10 / £20 for the programmes: low enough to remove purchase friction; high enough to filter out non-serious buyers and signal that the work has value. At these prices, conversion is more important than margin per sale.

Blog subscription anchoring: the £5/month founding offer creates a low-friction entry point. Once trust is established, subscribers upgrade to physical training plans (£10–£20) and mental performance plans (£10–£20).

Cross-plan companion pricing: physical and mental plans are priced identically. A client doing both pays £20 (two 6-week plans) or £40 (two 12-week plans) and gets a coordinated training and mental performance arc rather than two disconnected products.

Team programmes and workshops are paused for now. If/when relaunched, team programmes would target clubs and panels (20–30 athletes) at a per-athlete cost positioned against in-person S&C coaches; workshops would be revisited as live online events once the productised plans are at scale.

13. How to Scale Financially

Scaling for Mind the Gael is not about more products. It is about doing more of the same things, with the same per-sale margins, to more people. The platform's economics already work: every plan sale nets ~£9-£19 after costs, with no marginal staffing cost. The only thing standing between Year 1 numbers and Year 3 numbers is reach.

13.1 Three scaling levers, in order of impact

LeverWhat it changesCost to operateRealistic timeline
1. Content reach Drives every other metric. Each blog post / toolkit released is a permanent SEO and Instagram asset that brings in traffic for years. Monthly cadence already in place. Time only. Content is already drafted through Month 12. Compounds from Day 1. Reaches meaningful traffic at ~Month 6.
2. Subscription growth £5-£8 per subscriber per month with near-zero marginal cost. 100 subscribers = ~£600-£800/mo recurring. The low-friction entry that compounds over time. Already built. Stripe handles the billing. Year 1 target: 50 founding subscribers. Year 2: 150. Year 3: 300+.
3. Flip to v2 autonomous Removes Emily as the bottleneck on plan delivery. v1 routes every plan through her for review (capped at ~10 plans/week without burning out). v2 auto-delivers standard plans. Single env-var change. £0 to flip. The architecture is built. Once Emily has reviewed ~30 plans and is confident the output meets her standard, flip the env var.

13.2 What does NOT scale (avoid these traps)

  • 1:1 coaching at scale. Hourly capacity is a hard ceiling. Mind the Gael's whole architecture is built to avoid this trap.
  • More products before product-market fit. Adding new products before the current ones are converting at a known rate splits attention and dilutes data.
  • Heavy paid ads in Year 1. Without a strong conversion rate, paid ads pour money into a leaky bucket. Wait until organic conversion is measured before scaling paid.
  • Custom enterprise deals. Bespoke programmes for individual clubs at low volume eat time without scaling. Standardise first, customise later.

13.3 Where the next 1,000 customers come from

Realistic acquisition channels for Year 1 – Year 3, ordered by expected fit:

  1. Instagram organic. Every blog and toolkit gets a paired carousel post (already in content tracker). Female athletes are active on Instagram; the platform is built for it.
  2. LGFA & GAA partnerships. Specifically county Health & Wellbeing committees, GPA Player Development, county-level player liaison roles. Free content distribution in return for credibility.
  3. Word of mouth from buyers. Every plan recipient is a potential advocate, especially the early ones who experience the personalisation.
  4. SEO from the editorial calendar. 36+ articles across three series, all targeting search-relevant phrases ("how to deal with pressure in sport", "performance anxiety in female athletes", etc.). Compounds slowly but reliably.
  5. Email list to subscribers. Once the list crosses ~100, it becomes the highest-converting channel for new plan launches and price changes.

14. Summary Recommendation

Position: the platform is structurally profitable from day one. Variable costs per sale are negligible (<5 % of price). Fixed costs are near-zero until traffic crosses ~50k visits/month.

Focus: the limiting factor is not cost or technology — it is acquisition and conversion. Year 1 priority is marketing reach (Instagram, GAA / LGFA partnerships, partner clubs) rather than further product engineering.

Live products to scale: physical training plans and mental performance plans (parallel architecture, cross-linked companion field) are both live. Year-one priority is conversion and acquisition.

Three-year target: ~£40k cumulative net over Years 1-3, reaching ~£2,200/month net by end of Year 3 (~270 plan sales/month or equivalent mix). Pre-launch investment of ~£620 (~£435 of which is Anthropic API spend during the build, the rest subscriptions + domain) recouped within the first six months of moderate sales.
