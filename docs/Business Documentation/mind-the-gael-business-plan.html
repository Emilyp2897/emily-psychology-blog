Mind the Gael

Business Plan

AI-powered mental performance and physical training for female Gaelic footballers

June 2026 (revised)
Founder: Emily Phelan

1. Executive Summary

Mind the Gael is an AI-powered mental performance and physical training platform built specifically for female Gaelic footballers. The platform combines a library of evidence-based mental performance tools (the Gael Performance Toolkit) with AI-generated, personalised Mental Performance Plans and physical training plans, delivered through a modern web platform built on the Anthropic Claude API.

Ladies Gaelic football is one of the fastest growing female sports in Europe, with the LGFA reporting almost 200,000 members across roughly 1,000 clubs in Ireland and Britain. Yet sport psychology and structured strength and conditioning remain concentrated at county level. The ordinary club player, the vast majority of the playing population, has no affordable access to either. Mind the Gael closes that gap at a price point any club player can afford.

The platform is currently built and in v1 review mode, ahead of the 1 August 2026 content launch. The next milestone is an eight-week pilot with 15 players across four clubs, supervised by a sports psychologist, a strength and conditioning coach, and two to three personal trainers including the founder. The pilot will produce expert endorsements, named testimonials, and case studies that underpin the paid product launch later in 2026.

The business model combines low-friction one-off plan purchases with a content subscription: a £5 per month founding-offer subscriber blog (first 50 subscribers, then £8 per month standard), £10 for a 6-week plan and £20 for a 12-week plan, priced identically across mental performance and physical training. Phase 2 rolls the platform out to club memberships. The plan targets sustainable, founder-led growth funded from revenue, with modest startup costs because the founder builds the product herself.

Ladies Gaelic football is the proving ground, not the ceiling. The platform is built as a sport-agnostic engine: expert-reviewed content turned into AI-personalised plans for a specific sporting community, with Gaelic football as the first community. Once the model is proven against defined gate criteria, the same playbook extends to camogie and then to other women's sports.

Mission

To give every female Gaelic footballer access to the mental performance and physical training support that has, until now, been reserved for county panels.

2. The Problem

  • Mental performance support is rationed. Sport psychology in Gaelic games is concentrated in county setups. Club players dealing with match-day nerves, confidence dips, or focus problems have nowhere structured to turn.
  • Generic fitness apps do not fit the sport. Mainstream training apps are not built for the demands of Gaelic football: the mix of sprinting, kicking, contact, and the club training calendar. None are built for women in the sport.
  • The female player is underserved. Training and psychology content is overwhelmingly written for male athletes. Female players face distinct needs across physiology, injury risk profiles, and the realities of balancing sport with work and life.
  • One-to-one support does not scale or suit budgets. A sport psychologist costs far more per session than a club player will spend in a year. Clubs cannot fund it either.

Who feels this problem

The primary user is the adult female club player, aged 19 to 40, playing junior to senior club football in Ireland or Britain. She trains two to three times a week, plays matches through the summer, cares about performing, and has disposable income for sport at the price of a streaming subscription but not at the price of private coaching.

3. The Solution and Product

Mind the Gael is a web platform with four connected layers. Mental performance is the primary differentiator; physical training plans are a complementary product. The two product lines are cross-linked through an optional companion field on both intake forms, so a player doing both can have her mental plan reference her training schedule and vice versa.

Mental Performance Plan An AI-generated, personalised selection and sequencing of toolkit resources, matched to the player's competition level, performance moments to work on, peak moments, struggle moments, and schedule. Built on the Anthropic Claude API. Live (v1 review)

Why this product wins

  • Specificity. Built for one sport, one gender, one community. Generic competitors cannot match the relevance, and the niche is too small for big platforms to chase.
  • AI economics. Personalised plans at near-zero marginal cost (~£0.08-£0.16 per buyer) make individualised support viable at a club player's price point for the first time.
  • Expert-reviewed, AI-delivered. Toolkit content is reviewed by qualified professionals; the AI personalises and sequences it. The platform never positions itself as therapy or medical advice. Crisis disclosure routes the conversation to support services and to the founder rather than continuing as performance coaching.
  • Cross-linked products. The mental and physical plans are not silos. A player doing both has her mental plan anchored to specific training sessions and her physical plan layered with refocus cues and pre-performance routines.
  • Community roots. Founder-led, built from inside the GAA community, launched through founding clubs rather than paid acquisition.

5. Business Model and Pricing

The model combines one-off plan purchases with a content subscription. Plans are bought per training block rather than as an open-ended subscription, which matches how players actually think about a season (pre-season block, championship block) and keeps the buying decision small. Pricing is identical across the mental performance and physical training product lines.

Unit economics

  • Per-sale Anthropic API cost: ~£0.002 per teaser (Haiku) and ~£0.08-£0.16 per full plan (Sonnet, cached system prompt). Gross margin above 94% on plan sales.
  • A player buying one mental and one physical 12-week block spends £40 per season; an engaged blog subscriber adds £60-£96 per year.
  • The blog subscription is the recurring base and the conversion path: subscribers are the warmest buyers of plans at season milestones (pre-season, championship, return from injury).
  • One-off pricing removes churn risk on plans entirely; revenue seasonality is managed with January pre-season and May championship campaigns.
  • Hosting on Vercel and static architecture on Astro keep infrastructure costs near zero at this scale.

7. Expansion Strategy: Beyond LGFA

The scalable asset is not Mind the Gael the brand; it is the engine underneath it: expert-reviewed performance content, structured as a toolkit, turned into personalised plans by AI, launched into a sporting community through a pilot and founding clubs. Ladies Gaelic football proves the engine. Expansion replicates it.

Sport-agnostic architecture

  • Toolkit content, plan generation logic, and sport-specific knowledge are kept as separate layers. Core mental performance tools (attention control, confidence, routines) are sport-neutral; each sport adds a flavour layer of context, language, and examples.
  • Generation prompts treat sport, season structure, positions, and competition names as variables rather than hardcoded text. A sport-profiles.ts file contains 12 sport profiles with a General Female Athlete fallback for any sport not yet documented.
  • Result: launching a new sport is a content and community job, not a platform rebuild.

The expansion gate

Expansion begins only when the LGFA version has proven the model. Gate criteria, measured over a full season cycle:

  • Repeat purchase rate of 40% or higher among plan buyers
  • Average plan usefulness rating of 4.0 or higher
  • Blog subscriber to plan buyer conversion of 25% or higher per season
  • At least 4 club memberships sold and renewed intent confirmed
  • Expert review and AI guardrail process running with a report rate under 1%

Brand architecture

Mind the Gael remains the brand for Gaelic games, where the name carries community meaning, and stretches naturally to camogie. Expansion beyond Gaelic games will sit under a neutral platform identity, with Mind the Gael as its first and flagship product. No renaming is needed now; the principle is simply not to bind the Gaelic-specific name into structures that are costly to change, such as the company registration and the codebase.

Operating principles

  • Founder builds and operates the product; no salaried staff in year one.
  • Expert reviewers engaged on agreed terms per review cycle rather than employed.
  • AI guardrails: system prompts keep generators within performance coaching, with hard-stop crisis detection routing the user to support services and directly to the founder.

  • Quality loop: every reported output reviewed within 48 hours, with fixes shipped or logged.

9. Legal and Compliance

A full legal checklist exists as a separate working document (Mind the Gael Legal Compliance Checklist). The headline positions:

  • 18+ platform. Date-of-birth gate at signup (pending implementation), eligibility clause in terms, no marketing to underage players. This removes the minors exposure entirely, and the 19-40 target market loses nothing.
  • Not medical advice. Disclaimers at the point of use on both generators: AI-generated performance coaching, not medical, psychological, or physiotherapy advice, with support service signposting. Hard-coded into the system prompts.
  • Crisis hard stop. Already implemented in code: both chatbot and the training plan flow halt on any mental-health-crisis indicator, route the user to Samaritans / Pieta House, and notify Emily directly.
  • Citation rule. Hard-coded into every system prompt: any non-MTG content claim must be backed by a real named source (author + year). Hallucinated citations are explicitly forbidden.
  • GDPR (UK and EU). Privacy policy (pending) covering generator inputs, Anthropic API processing and international transfer, explicit consent for health-related inputs, retention, and user rights.
  • AI transparency. Clear labelling of AI-generated output, aligned with EU AI Act transparency obligations phasing in through 2026.
  • Anthropic compliance. Usage within Anthropic's Usage Policy and Commercial Terms for health-adjacent consumer products.
  • Solicitor review of terms, privacy policy, and disclaimers before paid launch.
