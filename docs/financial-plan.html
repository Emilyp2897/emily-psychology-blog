Online platform for mental wellbeing & performance coaching for female athletes

Founder: Emily Phelan

1. Executive Summary

Mind the Gael is an online platform delivering productised mental wellbeing and physical training content to female athletes. The business model is direct-to-consumer digital products with very high gross margins, supported by a small fixed-cost stack (Vercel, Postgres, Resend, Anthropic API, Stripe).

The revenue mix sits across four product families: personal training plans (one-off purchases), mental performance plans (one-off purchases, new product), workshops, and a monthly blog subscription. Each purchase costs the business roughly £0.40 to £0.90 in variable cost (API + payment processing), against retail prices that range from £5 to £30.

The platform is engineered to scale: the training plan generator runs autonomously after Stripe verification, content is served from a CDN-backed static site, and the live human intervention layer (Emily) is only invoked for clinical-pause cases and the four specialised training tracks (pregnancy, postpartum, endometriosis, return-to-play).

Headline numbers. Per-sale gross margin sits between 92 % and 99 %. Fixed monthly running costs at v1 scale are approximately £35/month (dominated by Canva and Claude Pro subscriptions). The business breaks even at 4 programme sales per month.

9. Cost Optimisations Available

  • Prompt caching — Anthropic discounts identical prompt prefixes when used within 5 minutes. Could shave ~50 % off input-token cost. Worth implementing once traffic justifies it.
  • Lower max_tokens caps — current 6,000 and 12,000 token caps are generous. Empirical usage will likely be 30–40 % lower. Caps can be tightened after testing.
  • Cheaper model for teasers — Claude Haiku is ~5× cheaper than Sonnet. The teaser is short and structured; could be moved to Haiku for ~80 % cost reduction on the non-buyer path.
  • Move Postgres to Supabase free tier if Neon (Vercel Postgres) pricing becomes unfavourable at scale.

11. Pricing Strategy Notes

Why £10 / £20 for the programmes: low enough to remove purchase friction; high enough to filter out non-serious buyers and signal that the work has value. At these prices, conversion is more important than margin per sale.

Blog subscription anchoring: the £5/month founding offer creates a low-friction entry point. Once trust is established, subscribers upgrade to training plans (~£10–£20), mental performance plans (~£10–£20), and workshops (£30).

Workshop pricing: all workshops priced uniformly at £30 (no installments). Simpler purchase decision; positions workshops as a slight upgrade from the £10/£20 plans.

Team programmes are paused for now. If/when relaunched, they would target clubs and panels (20–30 athletes) at a per-athlete cost positioned against in-person S&C coaches.

12. Summary Recommendation

Position: the platform is structurally profitable from day one. Variable costs per sale are negligible (<5 % of price). Fixed costs are near-zero until traffic crosses ~50k visits/month.

Focus: the limiting factor is not cost or technology — it is acquisition and conversion. Year 1 priority should be marketing reach (Instagram, GAA / LGFA partnerships, partner clubs) rather than further product engineering.

Next products to validate: mental performance plans (parallel to training plans) and the workshops, both of which can be tested with minimal additional fixed cost.
